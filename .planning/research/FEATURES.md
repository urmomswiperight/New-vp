# Feature Landscape

**Domain:** Tiered Subscription Offer Creation
**Researched:** 2024-07-31

## Table Stakes

Features users expect in any credible offer, especially for subscriptions.

| Feature | Why Expected | Complexity | Notes |
|---------|--------------|------------|-------|
| **Clear Value Proposition** | Users need to quickly understand what they are getting and why it's worth the price. | Low | Essential for initial engagement. |
| **Basic Access/Functionality** | The core product/service must be delivered as promised. | Medium | The foundation of any subscription. |
| **Reliable Support** | Users expect access to help for issues or questions. | Medium | Builds trust and retention. |
| **Flexible Billing Options** | Standard monthly/annual payment choices. | Low | Industry standard for subscriptions. |
| **Transparent Pricing** | No hidden fees, clear understanding of what each tier costs. | Low | Builds trust. |
| **Money-Back Guarantee (Basic)** | Some form of assurance against dissatisfaction. | Medium | Reduces initial purchase risk. |

## Differentiators

Features that set a tiered subscription offer apart, directly leveraging Hormozi's principles.

| Feature | Value Proposition | Complexity | Notes |
|---------|-------------------|------------|-------|
| **Comprehensive Value Stacking** | Each tier bundles multiple solutions that address all relevant customer problems, making the offer irresistible and the perceived value far outweigh the price. Higher tiers offer progressively more value. | High | Requires deep understanding of customer pain points and creative bundling. |
| **Crystal-Clear Dream Outcome Pathway** | Each tier explicitly maps to a clearer, faster, or more complete path to the customer's ultimate desired result. The offer is framed around achieving this outcome. | High | Needs strong messaging and product alignment. |
| **Tier-Specific Exclusive Bonuses** | Valuable additional components (e.g., premium content, 1:1 coaching, advanced tools, community access) that enhance the perceived value of higher tiers without devaluing the core product. | Medium | Bonuses should be highly relevant and desirable to the target audience for each tier. |
| **Risk-Reversal Guarantees (Performance/Conditional)** | Strong guarantees that go beyond simple money-back, offering performance-based assurances or conditions that make failure virtually impossible for the customer (e.g., "Achieve X results or double your money back"). | High | Requires confidence in product delivery and clear terms, significantly boosts conversion, especially for higher tiers. |
| **Strategic Scarcity & Urgency (for upgrades/promotions)** | Limited spots in premium tiers, time-sensitive bonuses, or cohort-based access to create a fear of missing out and encourage quicker upgrades. | Medium | Must be genuine and ethical to maintain trust. |
| **Personalized Paths/Coaching** | Higher tiers offering tailored guidance, direct access to experts, or custom solutions to accelerate the dream outcome. | High | High value, high cost to deliver, but justifies premium pricing. |
| **Exclusive Community Access** | Building a supportive, high-value network for premium subscribers, fostering collaboration and additional learning. | Medium | Increases stickiness and perceived value. |

## Anti-Features

Features to explicitly NOT build or common pitfalls to avoid in offer construction.

| Anti-Feature | Why Avoid | What to Do Instead |
|--------------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Vague Value Proposition** | Customers won't understand the benefits, leading to low conversion and high churn. | Clearly articulate the specific problems solved and results delivered by each tier. |
| **Undifferentiated Tiers** | If tiers offer similar value, customers won't see a reason to upgrade, or will choose the lowest tier. | Ensure distinct value jumps and clear dream outcome paths for each progressive tier. |
| **Meaningless Bonuses** | Bonuses that don't genuinely add value or aren't relevant to the dream outcome dilute the offer. | Only include bonuses that are highly desirable, support the dream outcome, and increase the perceived value. |
| **Weak/Standard Guarantees for High-Ticket** | A simple "30-day refund" for a high-investment tier doesn't adequately mitigate perceived risk. | Implement strong, risk-reversal, performance-based, or conditional guarantees. |
| **Fake Scarcity/Urgency** | Artificially limiting offers or deadlines without genuine reason damages trust. | Use genuine scarcity (limited capacity) and urgency (time-limited bonuses) to encourage action. |
| **Overwhelm of Options** | Too many tiers or confusing feature lists make decision-making difficult. | Keep tiered structures clear, concise, and focused on distinct value propositions. |
| **Focusing on Features, Not Benefits/Outcomes** | Listing features without explaining how they lead to the desired dream outcome is ineffective. | Always translate features into the benefits and the ultimate dream outcome they help achieve. |

## Feature Dependencies

*   **Dream Outcome Definition** → **Value Stacking** (Stacking must support the outcome)
*   **Value Stacking** → **Tier Differentiation** (Distinct value for each tier)
*   **Tier Differentiation** → **Bonus Selection** (Bonuses enhance specific tiers)
*   **Dream Outcome & Value Stacking** → **Guarantee Strategy** (Guarantees should align with promised results)
*   **Offer Completion** → **Scarcity & Urgency** (These are marketing triggers for a complete offer)

## MVP Recommendation

Prioritize:
1.  **Clear Dream Outcome for a Core Tier**: Identify the primary target customer and their single biggest desired outcome.
2.  **Robust Value Stack for Core Tier**: Build a compelling package of solutions that directly leads to the dream outcome, justifying a clear price point.
3.  **Strong, Risk-Reversal Guarantee for Core Tier**: Mitigate perceived risk to boost initial adoption.

Defer:
*   **Multiple Tiers with Complex Value Stacks**: Start with one strong, irresistible core offer and iterate. Add more tiers strategically once the core offer is proven.
*   **Aggressive Scarcity/Urgency**: Focus on value and trust first; these can be added for specific promotions later.
